%% ── Dissertation ────────────────────────────────────────────
\textbf{Dissertation research.}\enspace
My dissertation develops \emph{adaptive} multilevel Monte Carlo (MLMC)
estimators for elliptic stochastic PDEs
\(-\nabla\cdot(a(\mathbf{x},\omega)\nabla u)=f\),
where \(a\) is a log-normal random field.
Standard MLMC requires pre-specified mesh hierarchies and sample counts,
which is fragile when the coefficient is spatially rough.
I designed an online algorithm that selects level parameters by minimising
a cost-versus-variance trade-off estimated from a Gaussian process
surrogate, and proved optimal \(\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon^{-2}|\log\varepsilon|^2)\)
complexity without \emph{a priori} knowledge of coefficient regularity.
Implemented in C++/MPI with FEniCS and PETSc, the method achieves a
\(3\times\) wall-clock speedup over standard MLMC on a 3-D Darcy flow
benchmark with weak scaling to 512~cores.
This work will appear in the \textit{SIAM Journal on Scientific Computing}
(2024).

%% ── Secondary research ──────────────────────────────────────
\textbf{Optimization and machine learning.}\enspace
During a research internship at National Lab, I proved sharp
\(\mathcal{O}(k^{-1/2})\) convergence rates for stochastic gradient
descent under \((L,\sigma)\)-smoothness — strictly weaker than the
standard bounded-gradient assumption — and showed the rate is
unimprovable by constructing matching lower bounds.
This preprint is under revision for \textit{NeurIPS~2024} and informs
my long-term interest in theoretically grounded optimisation for
large-scale inverse problems.
